Authority Layer for Organizational Intent

We build infrastructure to make organizational commitments explicit, trackable, and legitimate. Not task management—authority infrastructure.

The Vision

Source of Truth

If it's not in Handled, it wasn't committed. We establish what was actually promised, by whom, and when—creating an authoritative record of organizational intent.

System-Level Intelligence

When commitments slip, we explain why the system broke—not who failed. We attribute outcomes to structural friction: dependencies, capacity constraints, and coordination latency.

Tamper-Evident Ledger

Every commitment, attestation, and renegotiation is timestamped, hashed, and append-only. We provide chain-of-custody verification for organizational decisions.

Founding Principles

Authority Is Observed, Not Declared

We infer de facto authority from patterns of commitment, confirmation, and endorsement—never from org charts. Real authority reveals itself through who initiates, who confirms, and whose commitments unlock downstream work.

Predictive Integrity Over Blame

We detect divergence early by observing whether reality is converging toward commitments. Absence of progress signals are delivered privately to commitment owners—giving them a correction window before broader visibility.

Graceful Renegotiation

When priorities shift, commitments can be deprioritized, superseded, or rescoped without penalty. We help organizations clean up "Authority Debt"—outdated commitments that would otherwise fossilize.

IC-First Design

Individual contributors own their commitments. Our AI acts as a personal coach, not surveillance. Health signals go to the person who made the commitment first—with time to act before escalation.

Chain of Custody

All attestations and linked evidence are cryptographically hashed in an append-only log. Disputes never delete attestations—they create new events. The full history is always preserved.

Legitimacy Infrastructure

We're not building task management software. We're building the coordination layer that establishes what happened, what was promised, and why outcomes occurred—with audit-grade integrity.
